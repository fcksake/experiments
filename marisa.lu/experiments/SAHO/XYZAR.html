XYZ Augmented Reality

Google selected winner as "Most Likely to Create Tangible and Lasting Change"

Designed as an educational tool, XYZ AR is a digital three-dimensional "whiteboard" that can be accessed through a mobile app that encourages collaboration. I teamed up with three engineering studnts to create a mobile platform that supports augmented reality and 3D drawing over a period of 24 hours.

The "whiteboard" is accessible via a mobile app coded with Vuforia in Unity, using the smartphone camera to detect the XYZ coordinates of the april tag wand. Using the tilt of the tags and the distance from the camera, the application allows for the user to move within 3D space. We also utilized Facebook's API so users can collaborate in private sessions.

Physical componenets utilize an arduino to communicate with the mobile app over bluetooth. The housing was lasercut and assembled with adhesive, designed to accomodate the april tag "cube" and hardware components. The smartphone camera detects the perspective/distance/change in distance to determine where the user is with the wand, and sends the coordinates to the mobile app.

I worked primarily on hardware, developing the design and integration of the wand componenent. In addition to this, I lead concepts of how the hardware communicates with the software throughout the process, exploring different triangulation processes.

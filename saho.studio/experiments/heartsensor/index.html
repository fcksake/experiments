iOT BPM Bowtie

WHAT IS IT?
A two week technical project where I made a 3D printed bowtie that measures your heart rate, displays it through a blinking LED light, and sends live BPM to be monitored in realtime from a website
IDEA
Students were required to make an iOT device with custom signal-processing code to calculate pulse. I thought a heart monitor bowtie would be funny; imagine wearing it to a date and it's broadcasting your heart beating faster.

The decision was largely inspired by data artist Mimi Onuoha and her explorations on 'weird' data's dichotomy with the everyday human experience. How much information is too much? When is it uncomfortable? What is private and public?
EXECUTION
Used a Particle Photon microcontroller to process signals from Adafruit's pulse sensor. My arduino code on the photon used beta discrimination to smooth the signals before calculating for pulse. Particle JS on the backend of a webpage running on my server grabbed the live BPM data from Particle's cloud.

Optical heart monitor app

WHAT IS IT?
An android app that turns on the phone's flash and takes in camera feed to calculates your BPM through your finger using computervision generated data.
IDEA
I wanted practice making a robust sensor system. Created, explored and evaluated various signal processing algorithms and combinations
EXECUTION
Wrote the app through Processing with the android library. Exported code from laptop to a school android on developer mode via USB. Tested the accuracy of my program against iOS apps on the appstore, and ultimately against the Professor's heart monitor.

  • Graph charts out variation in pixel brightness from the camera feed. The white graph is a smoother representation of the black graph's raw data because it utilizes beta discrimination optimized for smoothing as close to real time as possible. The combination of second-order derivative filtering crossing a dynamic 'zero' under averaged and predictive conditions of heart rate change allow for peak detection to determine BPM.

    (so that was fancy words for using beta discrimination to smooth the values fo raw data, calculating a mean within a smaller window of the data set and setting that as the new zero crossing. In the mean time, I was still detecting how 'sharp' the peaks in the graphs were — aka, how abrupt is the change in signals — through calculating second order derivative and analyzing whether that change was within certain expected parameters — aka 'predictive conditions' (where based off previous data recieved and externally established expectations of the human body) determine the probable validity of a heart beat 'detection'.

Mobile Step Counter

WHAT IS IT?
Android app that counts your steps
IDEA
To get more practice making robust sensor systems and working with android library.
EXECUTION
same execution as above project, but based off acclerometer and gyrometer instead.

  • Combination of peak finding (calculating derivative and change in derivative), standard deviation filtering (taking out edge cases that might otherwise throw off calculations), and dynamic zero crossing. A boolean parameter prevents double counting by only registering 1 peak for everytime the accelerometer value crosses the zero point.

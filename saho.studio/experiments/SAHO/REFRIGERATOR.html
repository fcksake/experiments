Refrigerator Concept

Carnegie Mellon Admission Design Project 2015

As much as people are quickly adapting to new technologies in their lives, most developing countries are still faced with poor electricity penetration. This leads to individuals, living especially in rural and poor setups, unable to employ the new technologies like their more affluent and urban counterparts.

The concept was meant to cater to those who need refrigerated medications or preserve food longer to avoid frequent treks to faraway markets. In countries like Liberia or Namibia, locations with long periods of sunlight, the refrigerator would serve useful. The refrigerator is essentially two concentric cylinders: the outer made of a porous material, likely a clay, and the inner made of a layered recycled aluminum for maximum surface area, structured much like a CPU cooler. The space between the two cylinders can be filled with sand or soil that would then be saturated with water. As the water evaporates, it cools the inner cylinder, able to maintain a consistent temperature around 43 degrees.
